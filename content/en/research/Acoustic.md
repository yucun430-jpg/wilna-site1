intro = """Acoustic perception and acoustic positioning are key technologies that utilize sound signals to understand the environment and determine the spatial location of sound sources. The former focuses on extracting information such as speech, events, and environmental conditions from audio (e.g., speech recognition, noise monitoring, abnormal sound detection), while the latter focuses on estimating the direction and location of sound sources based on multi-microphone arrays and the propagation characteristics of sound waves (e.g., TDOA, beamforming). Due to their low cost, wide coverage, and the absence of line-of-sight requirements, these two technologies have significant application value in scenarios such as smart homes, robot navigation, AR/VR spatial audio, and security monitoring."""

detailedIntro = [
"Perception extracts semantic and event information from sound signals to identify the content of speech, the category of environmental sounds, or abnormal sound events; the latter answers the question Where does the sound come from, estimating the direction and location of the sound source through time differences, phase differences, and energy distributions of multi-microphone reception. Typical systems use microphones or microphone arrays as the collection entry point. In real environments, they face interference such as noise, echoes, and reverberation. Therefore, they often combine noise reduction, echo cancellation, de-reverberation, and speech enhancement preprocessing to improve signal quality, and then construct features in the time-frequency domain or directly learn acoustic representations (such as Mel spectra, MFCC, or end-to-end deep representations) to achieve robust recognition of speech, sound events, and scene states. At the positioning level, classic methods include geometric solution based on time difference of arrival (TDOA) and direction focusing based on beamforming. They can also jointly model separation and tracking of sound sources in complex scenarios to support multi-sound sources and dynamic targets. In recent years, deep learning has further promoted end-to-end positioning and cross-scenario generalization, improving stability in conditions of strong reverberation, occlusion, and low signal-to-noise ratio through data-driven methods. Thanks to the advantages of low cost, no line of sight requirement, and all-weather operation, acoustic perception and positioning have been widely applied in far-field interaction of voice assistants and smart homes, environmental noise and sound event monitoring, robot auditory navigation and human-machine collaboration, spatial audio alignment in AR/VR, abnormal sound detection and sound source tracking in security scenarios, and are continuously evolving towards multi-modal fusion, edge real-time deployment, and privacy-friendly perception systems."
