intro = """Wireless sensing is a type of non-intrusive sensing technology that utilizes common wireless signals in the environment (such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, millimeter waves, LoRa, etc.) to see the states of people and objects. It achieves capabilities such as action recognition, gait analysis, vital sign monitoring, and spatial state perception by analyzing the subtle disturbances (such as CSI fluctuations, Doppler effect) caused by human activities and environmental changes during the propagation of the signals. Compared to wearable devices or cameras, wireless sensing has the advantages of no need for contact, privacy friendliness, all-day operation, and certain robustness in penetration and obstruction. Therefore, it has broad application prospects in smart homes, health monitoring, security, human-computer interaction, and smart cities. With the development of 5G/6G, the Internet of Things, and AI, wireless sensing is accelerating its evolution towards higher precision, stronger generalization, and edge real-time deployment."""

detailedIntro = [
"A typical wireless sensing system typically consists of: data acquisition and channel extraction (such as obtaining CSI from commercial network cards, or obtaining distance-velocity-angle information using millimeter-wave radars); signal preprocessing and feature construction (noise reduction, phase correction, time-frequency transformation, Doppler spectrum generation, subcarrier/antenna dimension fusion, etc.); and learning and inference modules (from traditional statistical learning to deep models such as CNN/RNN/Transformer). To enhance reliability in complex environments, more and more works introduce multimodal fusion, combining wireless information with visual, inertial, acoustic, or other radar information to complement and complement each other, achieving more robust sensing performance in conditions of occlusion, strong multipath, cross-room or low signal-to-noise ratio. The key challenges in current research mainly focus on generalization and transferability across devices and scenarios (distribution drift caused by different room layouts, hardware differences, and personnel differences), robustness brought by environmental dynamic changes, and edge deployment under real-time and energy consumption constraints. Correspondingly, transfer learning/self-supervised learning, domain adaptation, lightweight networks, and end-side inference optimization have become hot research directions. Looking forward to the future, with the popularization of 5G/6G new air interfaces, higher bandwidth and larger-scale antenna arrays, and continuous enhancement of AI model capabilities, wireless sensing is expected to play a key enabling technology role in unobtrusive interaction, contactless health, ubiquitous security and smart spaces, achieving higher precision, stronger usability and wider application deployment."
